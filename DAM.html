If You Make It Searchable, They Will Use It

File management is more than taxonomy and naming conventions - it's organizing everything in an intuitive way so everyone can quickly and easily find what they need. In my last position, keeping a strict file nomenclature and folder structure for the local server was essential for universal order. For the online asset portal, I learned to add the kinds of keywords and metadata that our teams would search for, and added protective copyrights as well since the images were publicly available.

Taxonomy & Nomenclature

On a local server, consistent file and folder nomenclature was essential for a large team to easily understand the contents of each. The ingestion process involved batch renaming and photo retouching where needed, with quick distribution to make files immediately available to the department. The next step was to mirror those assets to the public facing portal.       

Asset Portal

The company used a browser-based portal for distribution of images, logos and other branding documents to field staff, customers and the media. By applying keyword metadata at item level along with copyright information I ensured they were searchable and protected. Along with some site-optimizing tactics such as common conversion options and synonyms, I was able to increase user growth in two years from 1-2 users per month to 3-5 users per day.

Adobe AEM / DAM

I was designated the Subject Matter Expert role for brand and assets representing Marketing stakeholders on the website and database redesign and the migration to Adobe AEM / DAM. I advised and coordinated with developers, leadership and database teams on best practices and solutions to serve all channels. This also included creating thousands of custom assets for the new site.
