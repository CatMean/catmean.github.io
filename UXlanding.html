User Experience (UX)

"I... fight for the users!"
- Tron

"I've been doing this all my life," I thought.  

In graphic design the focus always seemed to be the aesthetics and surface results, however it always nagged me that some stakeholders didn't prioritize what I felt were basic tenets: Who, What, When, Where, Why and How. After my first month of UX class, I couldn't believe how ridiculously obvious and intuitive it all was to me. "I've been doing this all my life," I thought. From throwing parties to sending emails, I always felt an excessive need to anticipate how my audience would react. Though I feared being too remedial at times, it always felt safer to never assume a person's level of understanding.

Asset Bank - internal corporate site

The company used a browser-based portal for distribution of images, logos and other branding documents to field staff, customers and the media. Through interviews, examining customer journeys and identifying pain points, I was able to tailor the platform to typical scenarios. In addition, I used site-optimizing tactics like common conversion options and keyword synonyms. In the span of 2 years the user growth increased from 1-2 users per month to 3-5 users per day.

Anna Meyer - artist quick site 

Though not a true case study, I include this project because it was my first attempt at customizing a website. An artist friend had an upcoming exhibition and wanted a simple overview site. I had 2 days to get a free or relatively low cost basic landing site up quickly. Having only basic familiarity with HTML/CSS, I had to understand enough to modify and change a found template that seemed to serve her needs: presenting her contact information and her most impressive works while portraying her as a fun, spirited creative.
